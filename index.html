Waiau hazelnuts, shelled on site. Local pork, lamb, and venison. Akaroa salmon and charcuterie. Regional cheeses. A pear and chocolate torte. And Chemex brewed Lyttleton coffee. There's more, all fresh, simple and good.

There's Nicholas, he's the winemaker and married to Penelope, who looks after the business. Penelope's twin sister, Jo, helps on the sales front. With their parentsc Rod and Stacey, the family owns Black Estate. Together they grow wine, talk about the weather and fight the good fight.

It's summer, hit the beach. There was a warm sunny start to the growing season, then unseasonal rain and now we have a vital, vercy green vineyard with happy fruit set. Shoot thinning is on going and the vines are trimmed.
